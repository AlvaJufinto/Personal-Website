>	To be, or not to be, that is the question:
>	Whether 'tis nobler in the mind to suffer
>	The slings and arrows of outrageous fortune,
>	Or to take arms against a sea of troubles
>	And by opposing end them.

William Shakespeare (Hamlet Act III, Scene I) 

I do always think, should I live life the way I wanna live, or should I live it the ideal full of achievement way that I expect to live. I do always afraid of being a mediocre or a failure, and I do always want to be successful in the way that I want to be successful. But then again, is it really worth it? The energy, the time, the effort, the sacrifice, the risk, and the potential failure that I have to endure just to achieve that success. Is it really worth it?

Being a software developer in a SaaS company when I was really young. I was 17 and I was the youngest when I got there! That really gave a me a sense of accomplishment.

Being an organist at my local church, I'm not the best organ player, but I'm still the youngest organist in my local church until now (Feb 2026). That also gave me a sense of accomplishment, and a sense of being useful for the community.

Being a good student with good grades and achievements, that also gave me a sense of accomplishment, and a sense of being useful for my future.

But on top of that, I don't really know who I really am, and I don't really know what I really want in life. I do have some dreams and aspirations, but I'm not sure if they are really what I want, or just what I think I should want. I do have some passions and interests, but I'm not sure if they are really what I want to pursue, or just what I think I should pursue.

I'm in college now, I have dreams to win many competitions. But the reality? I only won 2 competitions in my whole life, and I don't think I can win more than that. I'm not the best player in this field, for real.

I met really great musicians along the way of my organist journey. I reallyyy wanna be like em, but I'm really sure it will take so many years, the sacrifice? Oh no, I don't think I'll handle it. Practicing for hours every day, having rehearsals every week, performing in concerts every month, and competing in competitions every year, going to many churches every week, and so on.

My routine is very hectic right now, and I don't think I can handle more than this. I have to do my assignments, projects, and exams for my college, I have to practice for my organist service. After college, I have to rush to church for rehearsals or services. That is very tiring for me. 

I feel like everyone is becoming something and being fullfilled in their life. Some of my friends maybe think I already have a good life, but in reality I'm still NOT fullfilled.
