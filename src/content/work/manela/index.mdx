## Overview

TemanNelayan (Manela) is a GEMASTIK XVIII project developed collaboratively with **Alwan Athallah Mumtaz**, achieving **4th place** in the competition.

The platform was designed to help fishermen identify potential fishing locations and fish species by combining satellite-derived oceanographic data with analytical and AI-assisted insights. A major focus of the project was maintaining usability in low-connectivity maritime environments where stable internet access is often unavailable.

---

## Responsibilities

- Conducted collaborative research on methodologies for predicting fish locations and species based on oceanographic indicators.
- Collected and processed:
  - Chlorophyll-a (CHL-A)
  - Bathymetry data
  - Sea Surface Temperature (SST)
- Utilized Google Earth Engine for large-scale satellite data acquisition.
- Processed and structured datasets using Python for analytical workflows.
- Built backend services using Node.js and Express.js.
- Contributed to offline-first architecture and low-bandwidth optimization strategies.
- Implemented partial rendering techniques to improve usability on low-end devices.
- Participated in field testing and validation directly with fishermen.

## Impact

The project demonstrated how satellite data and lightweight AI-assisted systems could support practical fisheries decision-making in real-world maritime conditions.

Key outcomes included:
- Supporting fishermen with accessible fishing zone insights
- Enabling usability in low-connectivity environments
- Optimizing performance for lower-end mobile devices
- Achieving approximately 80% successful validation during field testing for core predictions and functionalities
- Securing 4th place at GEMASTIK XVIII
