## Overview

LaperBang is a demand-driven routing platform designed specifically to support mobile street food vendors in real-time.

The platform was inspired by a common everyday problem: people often suddenly crave specific street foods or traditional snacks — such as batagor or es doger — but have difficulty locating nearby mobile vendors. LaperBang aims to bridge this information gap by helping consumers discover nearby vendors while simultaneously helping vendors reach actual areas of demand more efficiently.

The system creates a mutually beneficial ecosystem:
- Consumers can access desired food vendors more easily
- Vendors can optimize routes and reduce inefficient roaming

The platform primarily targets:
- Traditional mobile food vendors
- Residential complex communities
- Urban consumers such as office workers and university students

---

## Core Features

### Craving Signal

Consumers can simply press a button to send a real-time signal indicating that they want a specific type of food or snack.

The system aggregates these individual signals into collective demand data.

### Smart Demand Clustering

Demand signals are processed using geospatial clustering techniques to identify high-demand areas or “hotspots”.

The clustering process is powered by the DBSCAN (Density-Based Spatial Clustering of Applications with Noise) algorithm.

Key behaviors include:
- Detecting dense consumer request clusters
- Identifying meaningful demand zones
- Ignoring isolated requests categorized as noise

### Real-Time Vendor Matching

Once a hotspot is detected, nearby relevant vendors are matched and notified instantly.

The system helps vendors prioritize areas with actual demand rather than moving randomly without market visibility.

## Geospatial Clustering System

The clustering mechanism operates through the following workflow:

1. Consumers send craving signals through the application
2. The server stores location coordinates
3. DBSCAN analyzes spatial density patterns
4. High-density request areas are identified as hotspots
5. Vendors receive real-time notifications for nearby hotspots

Requests that do not form meaningful spatial density are treated as noise and ignored to avoid inefficient routing recommendations.
